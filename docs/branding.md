Choice(s):

- `ventapilot`
   - `venta` comes from Spanish `ventas` meaning `sales`. (So it directly connects to benta / selling / negosyo / revenue.)
   - `pilot` means:
   - a guide
   - someone (or something) that controls or assists navigation
   - in tech, often used to mean an AI assistant or system that helps you run something

Combined meaning: `VentaPilot`:

    - 👉 “Your sales co-pilot”
    - 👉 “An assistant that helps you run your store”
    - 👉 “A system that guides your business decisions”

So in a simple, intuitive interpretation:

VentaPilot = an AI-powered assistant that helps sari-sari store owners manage sales, inventory, debts, and business operations like a co-pilot.

Why the name works well

1. Very clear business context

- “Venta” instantly hints at selling / negosyo

2. Strong AI direction

- “Pilot” naturally implies future AI features (like co-pilot systems)

3. Emotional positioning
   Instead of “software” or “POS system,” it feels like:

- “may kasama kang tumulong magpatakbo ng tindahan mo”

4. Scalable
   It doesn’t lock you into sari-sari stores only:
   - groceries
   - pharmacies
   - small retail chains
   - even micro-warehousing

Brand personality it suggests
VentaPilot feels like:

- smart but friendly
- guiding, not intimidating
- assistant-like rather than “system-like”
- similar positioning to modern AI tools like “copilot” products

Simple tagline ideas

- “Your co-pilot for smarter sales.”
- “Run your store like a pro.”
- “From benta to business intelligence.”
- “Your sari-sari store, on autopilot.”
- “Smarter selling starts here.”

If you continue building it with AI features, the name fits especially well because you can naturally brand it like:

> VentaPilot AI: your business co-pilot for everyday retail.

Overall, it’s a solid choice—clear meaning, strong tech identity, and very expandable beyond just a POS app.
